Pork Adobo

Pork adobo is the beloved national dish of the Philippines.

Pork adobo is a quintessential Filipino dish that showcases the harmonious blend of salty, tangy, and garlicky flavors through the slow simmering of pork in a marinade of soy sauce, vinegar, and aromatics, resulting in a deeply savory and tender stew that epitomizes the comforting essence of Philippine cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Pork - shoulder/butt or or a combination of both
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Whole black peppercorns
  • Dried bay leaves
  • Water or broth

    • Some additional ingredients that may be used include:

  • Brown sugar
  • Onions
  • Salt

Recipe instructions

  1. Combine the pork (belly or shoulder/butt cuts are recommended), soy sauce, and crushed garlic in a bowl or pot. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour
  2. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the marinated pork and cook for a few minutes until lightly browned
  3. Pour in the remaining marinade along with whole peppercorns and dried bay leaves. Add water or broth (about 1 1/2 cups) and bring to a boil
  4. Once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork is very tender. Add more liquid if it dries out too much
  5. Pour in vinegar (about 1/4 to 1/2 cup, depending on your taste preference) and let it simmer for 12-15 more minutes to allow the vinegar to mellow out
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning by adding salt if needed
  7. Optionally, you can let the adobo rest overnight to allow the flavors to develop further. The next day, you can fry the pork pieces briefly to create a crispy exterior before serving
  8. Serve the adobo hot, with the sauce poured over the meat. It is traditionally eaten with white rice
